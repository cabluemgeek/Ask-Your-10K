### cybersecurity_2024
**Q:** What risks does Apple disclose about cybersecurity?

**A:** According to Item 1A of the 2024 Form 10-K under the heading "Risk Factors," [1][3], Apple Inc. discloses the following risks related to cybersecurity:

* The Company's business may be materially adversely affected by the outcomes of investigations, litigation, or changes to laws and regulations in the future.
* Changes to the Company's business practices to comply with new laws and regulations can negatively impact the reputation of the Company's products for privacy and security and otherwise adversely affect the experience for users of the Company's products and services.
* The Company relies on global suppliers that are also exposed to ransomware and other malicious attacks, which can disrupt business operations.
* Although the Company takes steps to secure confidential information provided to or accessible by third parties working on the Company's behalf, such measures may not always be effective, resulting in losses or unauthorized access to, or releases of, confidential information.

These risks highlight the potential for cybersecurity incidents to impact Apple's business, reputation, and financial condition.
